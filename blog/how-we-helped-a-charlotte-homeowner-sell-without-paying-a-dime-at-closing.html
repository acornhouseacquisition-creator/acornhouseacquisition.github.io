How We Helped a Charlotte Homeowner Sell Without Paying a Dime at Closing

April 28, 2026  ·  3 min read  ·  By Acorn House Acquisition

Marcus called us on a Tuesday afternoon. His voice was calm, but his situation wasn't.

He owned a 3-bedroom home in Cornelius, NC — just north of Charlotte. He bought it in 2021 for $338,000. By the time he called us, the home was worth about $319,000. His remaining mortgage balance was $312,000.

On paper, he had almost no equity. After agent commissions, closing costs, and three missed payments, he would have had to bring money to the closing table just to sell traditionally.

The bank had already sent a Notice of Hearing. He had weeks, not months.

The Problem With a Traditional Sale

A traditional sale would have looked like this:

  • Agent commission (6%): $19,140
  • Closing costs: $4,000–6,000
  • Three missed payments: $4,800
  • Total he'd need to bring to closing: roughly $21,000 out of pocket

Marcus didn't have $21,000. He had two kids, a new job in Raleigh, and a foreclosure date circled on his calendar.

What Is a Subject-To Deal?

A subject-to deal means we purchase your home while your existing mortgage stays in place.

Here's what that means in plain English:

  • We take ownership of your home at closing
  • Your mortgage loan stays at the bank in your name
  • We make your mortgage payments going forward
  • You walk away free and clear — zero money owed

The loan doesn't transfer to us. We take over the property and the payments. As long as payments are made on time, the bank has no reason to get involved.

Is This Legal?

Yes. Subject-to transactions are legal in North Carolina and across the US. Everything is done through a licensed title company with full documentation. We always recommend sellers consult with a real estate attorney before proceeding.

What Happened With Marcus

We walked Marcus through the subject-to option on that Tuesday call. By Thursday he had reviewed the terms with his own attorney. By the following Monday we were at the title company.

Here's how it broke down:

  • His mortgage balance: $312,000
  • What he brought to closing: $0
  • What he walked away owing: $0
  • Foreclosure: Stopped completely
  • Days from first call to closing: 9 days

We took ownership. We brought his missed payments current immediately. Marcus moved to Raleigh with a clean slate — no foreclosure on his record, no debt, no money lost.

Who Is This Right For?

This works best when:

  • You owe close to what the home is worth — or more
  • You can't afford the costs of a traditional sale
  • You're behind on payments and facing foreclosure
  • You need to move fast and can't wait 60–90 days for a listing
