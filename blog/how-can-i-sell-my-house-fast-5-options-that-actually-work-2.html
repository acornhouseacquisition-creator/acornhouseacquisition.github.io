How Can I Sell My House Fast? 5 Options That Actually Work

If you're asking "how can I sell my house fast" — you already know the traditional route isn't going to cut it. Listing with an agent, waiting for showings, negotiating with buyers, then sitting through a 45-day closing process is slow. Sometimes life needs a faster answer.

Here are 5 options that actually work, what each one costs you, and who each one is best for.

Option 1 — Sell to a Cash Buyer

This is the fastest path for most sellers. A cash buyer — usually an investor or investment company — makes you an offer directly, no bank involved. Closings typically happen in 7 to 21 days.

Best for: Sellers who need to move quickly, are behind on payments, or have a property that needs repairs.

What to expect:

  • Offer within 24 to 48 hours
  • No repairs required — sold as-is
  • Buyer typically covers closing costs
  • Offer may be below full market value in exchange for speed and certainty

Option 2 — Creative Financing (Subject-To)

A lesser-known option that works extremely well for sellers with an existing mortgage. Instead of the buyer getting a new loan, they take over your existing mortgage payments. You get cash at closing for your equity, and the deal closes fast.

Best for: Sellers who are behind on payments, have little equity, or need to avoid a lengthy bank approval process.

What to expect:

  • Faster close than traditional buyers
  • No need for the buyer to qualify for a new loan
  • All closing costs typically paid by the buyer
  • Great option if foreclosure is a concern

At Acorn House Acquisition, this is our specialty. We structure offers around your existing financing so the process is smoother and faster for everyone.

Option 3 — Price It Aggressively and List

If you have a little more time but still want a fast sale, pricing your home 5 to 10 percent below market value will generate multiple offers quickly — often within days. You still go through the traditional process, but a competitive price dramatically shortens the timeline.

Best for: Sellers who want to use an agent but need to sell within 30 to 45 days.

What to expect:

  • Multiple offers possible in the first week
  • Still requires inspections, appraisals, and bank approval
  • You'll likely leave some money on the table vs. waiting for full price

Option 4 — iBuyer Programs

Companies like Opendoor or Offerpad will make you an instant online offer based on an algorithm. It's fast and convenient, but the offers often come with service fees of 5 to 8 percent on top of a below-market price.

Best for: Sellers who want a guaranteed offer without any negotiation or showings.

What to expect:

  • Offer in minutes online
  • Fees can add up to more than a traditional commission
  • Limited availability depending on your market and property type

Option 5 — Sell to a Family Member or Known Buyer

If someone in your network has expressed interest in your property, a private sale skips agents, listings, and most of the waiting. You'll still need a title company and a real estate attorney to handle the paperwork properly.

Best for: Sellers who already have a buyer in mind.

What to expect:

  • Fastest possible close if both parties are motivated
  • No commissions
  • Still needs proper legal documentation to protect both sides

So Which Option Is Right for You?

Here's a simple way to decide:

  • Need to close in under 30 days → Cash buyer or creative financing
  • Behind on payments or facing foreclosure → Creative financing / subject-to
  • Home needs major repairs → Cash buyer, sold as-is
  • Have 30 to 45 days and want more money → Aggressive pricing with an agent
  • Want convenience above all else → iBuyer
