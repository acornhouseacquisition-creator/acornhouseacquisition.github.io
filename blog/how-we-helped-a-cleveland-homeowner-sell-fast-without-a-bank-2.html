How We Helped a Cleveland Homeowner Sell Fast — Without a Bank

April 28, 2026  ·  2 min read  ·  By Acorn House Acquisition

How We Helped a Cleveland Homeowner Sell Fast — Without a Bank

Not every home sale fits the traditional mold. Sometimes the numbers don't work for a conventional buyer, the timeline is too tight, or the seller just needs a solution that a bank can't provide.

This is the story of how we helped one homeowner in Cleveland, Ohio close quickly — without any of the usual friction.

The Situation

The seller owned a 4-bedroom, 2-bathroom home in a prime downtown Cleveland location. The property was in great shape — 2023 architectural roof, newer HVAC, updated kitchen. On paper it was a strong asset.

But there was a challenge. The seller had an existing mortgage with a low 2.9% interest rate and needed to move quickly. A traditional sale would take 45 to 60 days minimum, require a new buyer to qualify for financing at today's higher rates, and add weeks of uncertainty neither side wanted.

They needed a different kind of buyer.

What We Did

We came in with a creative subject-to offer — meaning we took over the seller's existing mortgage rather than replacing it with new financing. Here's how the structure looked:

Purchase Price: $258,607 Existing Mortgage We Took Over: $208,353 at 2.9% interest Monthly PITI: $1,607 Entry Fee Paid to Seller: $28,000 at closing

The seller also had a solar loan on the property — $22,254 at 1.9% with a $142 monthly payment — which we folded into the structure as well.

Total monthly payment all-in: $1,749.

Why It Worked for the Seller

The seller walked away with $28,000 cash at closing without waiting for a traditional buyer to get bank approval. No repairs required. No showings. No uncertainty about whether the deal would fall through at the last minute.

For a seller who needed speed and certainty, this was a better outcome than listing and waiting.

Why It Works as an Investment

The property sits in a prime downtown Cleveland location — everything within walking distance. Market rent runs around $2,670 per month according to RentCast. With total monthly payments of $1,749, the long-term cash flow is approximately $1,000 per month.

Run it as a co-living strategy — renting by the room — and net cash flow jumps to $2,000 to $3,000 per month.

A 2.9% interest rate locked in, positive cash flow from day one, and a seller who got exactly what they needed.

The Takeaway

Creative financing isn't a workaround — it's a legitimate structure that creates real wins for sellers who need speed and flexibility, and investors who understand how to use it.
